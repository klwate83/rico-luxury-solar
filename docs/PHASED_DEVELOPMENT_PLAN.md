# RICO Luxury Solar Energy — Phased Development Plan

## Phase 0 — Discovery and governance

**Goal:** Confirm business, brand, legal and integration decisions before development.

Deliverables:
- Final scope and user journeys
- Confirmed launch service areas
- Approved brand assets and tone
- Verified contact information
- Partner-vetting criteria
- Data fields and consent wording
- CMS roles and approval workflow
- CRM selection
- Analytics measurement plan
- Content inventory

Exit criteria:
- Signed-off sitemap, wireframe direction and data model
- Confirmed production owners for content, leads and partner applications

## Phase 1 — Technical foundation

**Goal:** Establish the production-ready project structure.

Deliverables:
- Next.js, TypeScript and Tailwind project
- Git repository and branching model
- Vercel staging deployment
- Cloudflare DNS/security plan
- Environment-variable management
- Shared component library
- Sanity CMS project and schemas
- Base SEO metadata
- Error, loading and empty states

Exit criteria:
- Staging site accessible
- CI build passes
- CMS draft and publish flow verified

## Phase 2 — Core website build

**Goal:** Launch the premium informational and conversion experience.

Pages:
- Home
- About
- Residential Solar
- Commercial Solar
- Battery Storage
- Maintenance and Upgrades
- How It Works
- Contact
- Quick Quote
- Partner With Us
- Thank You
- Legal pages

Deliverables:
- Responsive navy/gold design system
- Mobile hamburger navigation
- Sticky mobile conversion actions
- Quick-quote experience
- WhatsApp, phone and email actions
- Rule-based chatbot
- Trust and partner sections
- CMS-managed page content

Exit criteria:
- Desktop, tablet and mobile QA passed
- All conversion paths tested

## Phase 3 — Lead automation and CRM

**Goal:** Turn form submissions into operational lead workflows.

Deliverables:
- HubSpot or Zoho integration
- n8n workflows
- Lead-reference generation
- Confirmation emails
- Internal notifications
- Lead-source and UTM attribution
- Customer and partner pipelines
- Consent record storage
- Lead-status model
- Basic lead-scoring rules

Exit criteria:
- Test lead appears correctly in CRM
- Email and internal alert received
- Attribution data retained
- Failure and retry handling tested

## Phase 4 — SEO, content and local visibility

**Goal:** Establish initial organic acquisition capability.

Deliverables:
- Sanity article workflow
- Four launch articles
- Five to six optimised core pages
- XML sitemap and robots rules
- Schema markup
- Google Search Console
- Google Business Profile optimisation support
- Local Cape Town keyword mapping
- Internal-linking structure

Exit criteria:
- Site indexed
- No critical technical SEO issues
- Content approval workflow operational

## Phase 5 — Analytics and CRO

**Goal:** Measure and improve conversion performance.

Deliverables:
- GA4
- Google Tag Manager
- Conversion event taxonomy
- Form-start and form-complete tracking
- WhatsApp, email and call tracking
- Chatbot tracking
- Dashboard for traffic, source and conversion
- Baseline funnel report
- First CRO backlog

Exit criteria:
- All priority events visible in analytics
- Test traffic attributed correctly

## Phase 6 — Paid media readiness

**Goal:** Prepare the website for controlled Google Ads scale-up.

Deliverables:
- Reusable campaign landing-page template
- Campaign-specific headlines and forms
- UTM and GCLID storage
- Conversion-tracked thank-you pages
- Google Ads conversion configuration
- Ad-to-page message consistency checklist

Exit criteria:
- Landing-page template approved
- End-to-end paid conversion test completed

## Phase 7 — Partner monetisation and marketplace expansion

**Goal:** Add commercial partner-management capability after lead quality is proven.

Potential deliverables:
- Partner plans and commercial rules
- Pay-per-lead or pay-per-sale tracking
- Partner assignment rules
- Partner portal
- Lead acceptance and rejection
- Partner performance dashboard
- Invoice and commission data
- Advanced lead routing
- Additional trade vertical architecture

Entry condition:
- Consistent qualified lead volume
- Agreed lead-quality definition
- Validated commercial model

## Recommended release sequence

**MVP release:** Phases 0–3

**Growth release:** Phases 4–6

**Marketplace release:** Phase 7

## Roles

- Maendeleo Solutions: architecture, development, deployment and integration
- Zimkhitha / Marketing Director: campaign strategy, content governance and brand alignment
- Client owner: approvals, legal content, business rules and lead ownership
- Solar partners: vetting data, service capability and lead outcome feedback

## Definition of done

A feature is complete only when:
- It works on desktop and mobile
- It has validation and error handling
- Analytics events are defined
- CMS ownership is assigned where relevant
- Security and consent requirements are met
- QA evidence is recorded
- Client approval is captured
